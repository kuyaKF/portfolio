FAQ
what is aCA and who is eligible for it?
ACA, also known as Obamacare, is a comprehensive health care reform law that was passed in the United States in 2010. It aims to improve access to affordable health care for all Americans, regardless of income or pre-existing medical conditions. ACA offers a variety of insurance options to individuals, families, and small businesses through state and federal marketplaces, also known as exchanges.
Eligibility for ACA depends on factors such as income, citizenship status, and age. Generally, individuals who are legal residents of the United States and who do not have access to affordable health insurance through their employer or a government program like Medicaid may be eligible to purchase insurance through ACA exchanges.
